<h2>Psychological treatments</h2>
<h2>Cognitive Behaviour Therapy(CBT)</h2>
Cognitive behaviour therapy (CBT) is a kind of structured psychotherapy that aims to teach us to think life and ourselves in a more positive way based on the understanding that thinking negatively is a bad habit.

&nbsp;Cognitive behaviour therapy (CBT) is efficacious in the prevention of relapse or recurrent episodes of depression. When we experience depression, it can help us correct the thinking patterns that we do not notice errors of judgement caused by thinking ourselves and the world in a negative way.

<h2>interpersonal psychotherapy (IPT)</h2>
Interpersonal psychotherapy is diagnosis-targeted, time-limited, present-focused treatments that encourage us to regain control of mood and functioning.

IPT makes a practical link between the mood and disturbing life events that either trigger or follow from the onset of the mood disorder.

It aims to help us understand how our vulnerabilities can result in the risk of developing depression in the future.
